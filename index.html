Bio /

Richard Rojas is a Digital Media Art student at San José State University whose work moves between 3D animation, 2D animation, graphic design, UI/UX design, motion graphics, video, and experimental technology. His practice is shaped by digital culture, music, lighting, character-driven visuals, and the feeling of entering a space that feels both futuristic and personal.


Through tools like Blender, After Effects, Clip Studio Paint, interface design, and interactive media systems, Richard builds visuals that explore atmosphere, rhythm, usability, and digital presence. His work often connects technical process with emotional worldbuilding, where the software, screen, movement, and viewer all become part of the final experience.

Artist Statement

My work explores the intersection between technology, music culture, digital space, and visual art. I work across 3D animation, 2D animation, graphic design, UI/UX design, motion graphics, video, and interactive systems because I like the idea of not being locked into one medium. I’ll basically say that my art comes from trying to build a space where visuals, sound, movement, and technology can all connect with each other.


A lot of my inspiration comes from music videos and rave visuals, especially the way Daft Punk music videos use technology, helmets, light, performance, and identity to make something feel iconic. I also take inspiration from S3RL and rave music because of the speed, color, rhythm, and emotional exaggeration that comes from that kind of sound. For my 3D work, I see inspiration from Andrew Blanton’s digital media practice and how technology can become part of the artwork itself, not just a tool behind it. My work also connects back to electronic music culture, stage lighting, internet aesthetics, animation, and the feeling of being inside a visual system.


In my own practice, I use programs like Blender, After Effects, Clip Studio Paint, and design tools to create digital forms, animated scenes, interface layouts, graphic work, and experimental visual compositions. I also love using my XP-Pen tablet to create a greater degree of digital painting work, with some of my favorite parts being anatomy studies and doing line art for others. Outside of my school practice, I have also built an online persona under the name S0UNDB1T3, where I share my creative work and visual identity across TikTok and other platforms. That online presence has grown to over 5.5k followers, which also connects back to how I see art, branding, performance, and digital culture working together.

Creative Practice

3D / 2D Animation

I create 3D and 2D animation through character movement, timing, lighting, composition, and digital worldbuilding. Animation is a big part of my practice because it lets me make the image feel alive, not just still on the screen.

Motion Graphics / Video

My motion graphics and video work focus on rhythm, transitions, typography, atmosphere, and music-driven editing. I like when visuals move with the same kind of energy as rave music, music videos, stage lighting, and animated digital culture.

Graphic Design / UI/UX

I work with graphic design and UI/UX through layout, branding, interface flow, typography, and visual systems. The goal is to make the screen feel clean, readable, and immersive while still keeping my own visual identity in it.
